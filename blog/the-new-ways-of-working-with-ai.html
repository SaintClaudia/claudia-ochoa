The New Ways of Working with AI: Redefining Productivity, Creativity — and Vibe Coding

July 10, 2025

AI is no longer a buzzword — it’s now an integral collaborator reshaping how we work, create, and solve problems. From freelancers to Fortune 500 companies, everyone is learning how to harness AI to do more, think bigger, and focus on what humans do best: innovate.

From Web Designers to Vibe Coders

Design is a perfect example of how roles evolve alongside technology. We started as designers who coded, then visual designers, then UX/product designers — and now, I say, vibe coders. Designers once spent hours arranging static pixels. Today, AI helps wireframe, prototype, and generate visuals in seconds.

Product managers are using tools like v0.dev to create prototypes instantly. Designers use the same tools to wireframe ideas faster than ever. It raises a real question: will we still use static design tools like Figma in the future? Or will designers simply vibe code — prompting AI to generate interfaces from design systems?

The real advantage will go to the tools that can tap into robust design systems — and the designers who know how to prompt them. In this world, the designer’s role shifts from pushing pixels to directing feelings and orchestrating experiences that resonate.

Knowing People is Knowing AI

As Ethan Mollick puts it, “If you know people, you will know AI.” AI is trained on our words, ideas, and patterns — it mirrors us. So the secret to using AI is still deeply human: understand people. The best results come when humans bring context, taste, and emotion to guide AI’s speed and recognition.

A New Team

This is what makes AI so exciting: it doesn’t erase human skills — it amplifies them. We still need critical thinking to check AI’s output. We need creativity to push past generic ideas. We need empathy to craft products that people truly love.

The new ways of working are already here. The question is not whether AI will replace you — but how you will make AI your most valuable collaborator. Those who embrace it, direct it, and pair it with human insight will shape what comes next.

As Kevin Weil once said, “It’s not AI that will replace your job — it’s the person who uses AI.”
